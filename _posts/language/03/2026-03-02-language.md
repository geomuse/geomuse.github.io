# AI Fears End 20-Year Poetry Contest in Japan

A poetry competition in Japan has ended after 20 years because organizers were worried that some poems were written using AI.

The competition asked people to write senryu, a type of Japanese poem. Senryu are similar to haiku and usually have three lines with a 5-7-5 syllable pattern.

However, the topics are different. Haiku usually describe nature and the four seasons. Senryu, on the other hand, focus more on people and daily life, and often have jokes in them.

The event has taken place every year since 2006 in Sakaiminato, a city in Tottori Prefecture. The competition was started as a way to remember the late manga artist Shigeru Mizuki, who was from Sakaiminato.

Mizuki is famous for his manga series GeGeGe no Kitaro, which includes many well-known yokai — monsters from Japanese folklore.

For this reason, the competition asked writers to make senryu poems about yokai.

Since last year, however, organizers have noticed something strange — many of the poems had very similar patterns and jokes. Some poems even used exactly the same words!

Toyohiro Yuki, head of the tourism organization in Sakaiminato, said it had become very hard to know whether the poems were written by people or made by AI.

"Distinguishing between the two is extremely difficult," he told The Japan Times.

Because of this, the organizers felt they could no longer choose the competition's winners fairly, so they decided that it will not take place next year.

Now, Sakaiminato's tourism organization is looking for new events that could bring more visitors to the city.
